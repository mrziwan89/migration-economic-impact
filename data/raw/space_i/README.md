## Description
SPACE I is the **definitive annual census of prison populations across all Council of Europe member states** — covering 47 countries including all EU27 nations plus the UK, Switzerland, Norway, Turkey, and others. Published annually since 1983, it collects standardized data from prison administrations on the number and characteristics of incarcerated individuals, making it the European equivalent of the U.S. Bureau of Justice Statistics' National Prisoner Statistics (NPS) program.

The 2024 edition specifically highlights trends including **rising foreign inmate populations**, and includes the percentage of **foreign nationals** (non-citizens) in each country's prison system — the key variable for this project's fact-checking analysis.

## Project Role
> **Core Dataset — Research Objective 4 (Fact-Checking Safety)**

This report is the **European counterpart to the BJS NPS dataset**, and together they enable a transatlantic fact-check on immigrant incarceration:

1. **Fact-Checking Safety (EU side):** Provides the share and count of foreign nationals in European prisons, allowing per-capita incarceration rates to be calculated for citizens vs. non-citizens across European countries.
2. **Cross-Regional Comparison:** Paired with BJS NPS data (US), this enables a direct **US vs. Europe comparison** of how immigrant incarceration rates compare to native-born rates in each region.
3. **Trend Context:** The rising foreign inmate population trend noted in the 2024 edition can be contextualized against rising foreign-born population shares — showing whether rates are actually increasing or just tracking population growth.

## Usage Notes
- This is a **PDF report**, not a raw data file. Key statistics must be **manually extracted** from the tables or sourced from the accompanying Excel annexes available on the SPACE website.
- For analysis, download the **Excel data annex** from [coe.int/space](https://www.coe.int/en/web/prison/space) to get machine-readable versions of the tables.
- "Foreign inmates" in SPACE I = **non-nationals** (persons without citizenship of the imprisoning country) — comparable to BJS "non-citizen" category.
- Per-capita rates should be computed using Eurostat population data by citizenship to make fair citizen vs. non-citizen comparisons.
- Foreign inmate percentages vary enormously by country (e.g., Luxembourg ~70%, Eastern EU countries ~5%) due to differences in national demographics, not necessarily enforcement differences.
